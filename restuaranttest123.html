About the Web App:

This app was designed to work equally well on desktops, tablets and smartphones. On smartphones and tablets, the functionality is similar to native apps except that it is used in the browser. Just bookmark the webpage in the browser instead of downloading anything. An internet connection is always required.

This web app uses Google data for the restaurants listed. They are displayed in order of prominence, which can be affected by the ranking of the place in Google's index, global popularity and other factors. So essentially the most popular results are listed first as well as the most relevant results. Some restaurant headings will return results that don't quite fit because the business owner's listed their establishment inaccurately and/or other factors. Only up to 60 restaurant for each heading will be displayed.

Is there a problem with information? If you see inaccurate results or something that shouldn't be there, you can follow the link to the Google+ page to report a problem, follow the link on the map or you can check out this page. As this app relies on Google data, any inaccurate information has to be reported to Google.

Using the App:

Clicking on the restaurant headings will retrieve results for that type of restaurant and display them on the map. You can click the restaurant names in the list to get more information about that restaurant. You can also click the markers to bring up the same information. If you want to find a specific kind of restaurant in a specific part of town, click a restaurant heading and then either zoom to that part of town or use the quick zoom feature to see the restaurants in that part of town. Certain restaurant headings with many restaurants could take a couple of seconds to load all of them during which time the buttons are disabled. This app does not work with javascript disabled.

Business Owners:

For an establishment to be found on this app, the establishment has to be entered into Google Places for Business, which is free. To do that you can follow this link to Google Places for Business. The restaurant headings are really just a function of how many restaurants there are for a certain region, be it continent or country. The architecture of the app is geared towards region and place so you should attempt to be found that way. The goal of the app is to be as inclusive as possible while still being easy to navigate. If you feel your establishment is really falling through the cracks, for instance it is cuisine from a country that is not especially popular in the U.S., then it may be possible to include a separate heading. If a restaurant is not being found in the top results or is not in the top 60 results, there is no way to change that in this app.
